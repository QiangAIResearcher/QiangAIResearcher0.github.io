Direct Feedback Alignment Provides Learning in Deep Neural Networks

Artificial neural networks use back-propagation to update weights along the same way with the forward direction, which seems not biologically plausible. This paper examined a differrent approach, feedback alignment, to do the gradient descent and weight updating. As shown in figure 1, the output error is connected to the hidden layers in direct and indirect ways. Essentially, the neuiral networks take the error as the input, and treat the backward process as the forward process. No weight initialization is required.

Experiments were conducted on the MNIST and CIFAR. Comaprable performance was achieved. 18/12/2017

A Joint Model for Question Answering and Question Generation

This paper jointly learns a attention-based generative model for two tasks: question generation and question answering. The model encodes the document and the context (indeicating Q-gen or A-gen) to obtain answers given question or questions given answers. The pointer-softmax mechanism learns to switch between between copying words from the document and generating words from a prescribed vocabulary. The promising phenomenia includes a higher performance for for the answer generation, which is probably because that the answers produced by the joint model are highly related by the generated questions. This paper could be enhanced by including more explaination of experiment results and error analysis.

By alternating the input of question generation and answer generation, this paper inspires us to sovle problems by joint training. The beneficial intreaction between two tasks through parameter sharing could help imrpove each task under a multitask setting. 4/12/2017
